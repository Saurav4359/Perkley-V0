# Perkley - Validation & Proof of Work Plan

## Core Thesis

Creator marketing today is broken.

Brands:

* Spend significant time finding creators
* Negotiate individually
* Don't know which creator will actually perform

Creators:

* Struggle to get discovered
* Compete on follower count instead of performance
* Rarely get opportunities from brands

Perkley aims to change creator marketing from:

"Brands choose creators"

to

"Creators compete and performance decides winners."

# Perkley — Product Concept

## Vision

Build the world's largest **performance-based creator marketplace** — where brands don't hire creators, they launch bounties and let creators compete.

Instead of paying creators upfront based on follower count, brands reward **actual performance**.

Small creators with great content can outperform large creators and win.

---

## The Problem

### For Brands

Influencer marketing today is painful. Brands must:

- Search for creators
- Send DMs
- Negotiate prices
- Manage contracts
- Track posts manually
- Verify performance
- Handle payments

**Result:** slow, expensive, and difficult to scale.

### For Creators

Most creators never get opportunities. The current ecosystem favors:

- Large follower counts
- Existing relationships
- Agencies

**Problems:**

- Small creators get ignored
- Talent matters less than audience size
- No merit-based system exists

A creator with 5k followers may generate more sales than one with 500k — yet brands never discover them.

---

## The Solution

**Brands launch bounties. Creators compete. Performance determines rewards.**

The platform handles:

| Capability | What it does |
|---|---|
| Discovery | Connect brands with eligible creators |
| Verification | Confirm content meets campaign rules |
| Tracking | Measure views, leads, sales, and more |
| Payments | Distribute rewards automatically |
| Leaderboards | Rank creators in real time |
| Escrow | Lock funds until outcomes are verified |

---

## Core Insight

Brands care about **outcomes** — not followers, influencer status, or blue ticks.

If a creator generates sales, leads, installs, or reach, they should be rewarded.

---

## How It Works

### Step 1 — Brand Creates Campaign

The brand specifies budget, objective, eligibility, duration, and reward structure.

**Example campaign**

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Campaign | Protein Supplement Launch |
| Budget | ₹100,000 |
| Requirements | Fitness creators · Instagram · India-based · Min. 5k followers |
| Duration | 14 days |

### Step 2 — Funds Enter Escrow

The brand deposits campaign funds. Money is locked in escrow so creators know rewards are guaranteed.

### Step 3 — Creators Join

Eligible creators click **Participate** and receive:

- Campaign details and rules
- Brand assets
- Tracking links
- Referral codes

### Step 4 — Create Content

Creators publish reels, shorts, TikToks, tweets, or posts — depending on campaign requirements.

### Step 5 — Verification

The platform verifies:

- Content exists and is live
- Correct hashtags and links are used
- Submitted account matches the registered creator

### Step 6 — Leaderboard Updates

Creators see rankings in real time. Competition drives engagement, retention, and sharing.

### Step 7 — Winners Paid

When the campaign ends:

- Winners are determined automatically
- Escrow is released
- Earnings are deposited to creators

## Bounty Types

### 1. Awareness Bounty

**Goal:** Maximum visibility.

**Metrics:** Views · Reach · Shares · Saves · Engagement

**Example:** A new startup launches a product and rewards creators who generate the most awareness.

### 2. Referral Bounty

**Goal:** Generate signups.

Creators receive referral links and coupon codes. Leaderboard ranks by verified referrals.

**Example:** ₹50 per signup.

### 3. Sales Bounty

**Goal:** Generate revenue.

**Example:**

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Product price | ₹1,000 |
| Creator commission | 5% |
| Creator earns per sale | ₹50 |

### 4. Hybrid

Combine prize pool, referral rewards, and revenue share.

**Example:** Top creators win cash prizes; everyone earns commission on sales.

## Risks

### Marketplace Risk

Need brands and creators simultaneously — the classic chicken-and-egg problem.

### Fraud Risk

Potential abuse: fake followers, purchased engagement, fake referrals. Requires fraud detection.

### API Risk

Social platforms limit access. Verification may become difficult over time.

### Brand Safety Risk

Low-quality content, misleading claims, offensive material — moderation systems are required.
